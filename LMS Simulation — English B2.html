Reveal Analysis

Choose which LMS analysis to show to the class. This will highlight the key observations inside each environment.

⚠ LMS A — What Goes Wrong

📁

47 files with no structure

Content is uploaded without sequence, labels, or purpose. Students cannot tell what is essential, what is optional, or what order to follow. This creates cognitive overload before learning even begins.

🔗

No connection between online and live moments

The LMS and the Zoom class are parallel, not connected. Students consume online content and attend class — but neither moment builds on the other. This is not blended learning.

💬

The forum is used for logistics, not learning

Students use the discussion forum to ask about deadlines and find missing files — not to discuss ideas or practice language.

📊

Grades without feedback

Students receive scores but no explanation of what they did well or what to improve. Assessment becomes a label, not a learning tool.

✓ LMS B — What Works and Why

🔁

Clear Before → During → After learning cycle

Every week has three connected moments. The video prepares students for the live session; the live session applies what they prepared; the audio recording extends learning after class. Nothing is disconnected or repeated.

🎯

The home page is a compass, not a folder

Students know exactly where they are, what to do next, and why. The instructor communicates regularly with specific, purposeful announcements.

💬

The forum generates real interaction and language use

The discussion prompt requires students to produce language, express a position, and respond meaningfully to a classmate. The instructor participates with targeted feedback.

📝

Feedback is specific, timely, and feed-forward

Every piece of feedback tells students what worked, what to improve, and how that connects to the next task. Assessment becomes part of the learning design.
